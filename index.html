I am a researcher and faculty member at the intersection of Edge AI Security, Hierarchical Federated Learning, and Assistive Human-Machine Intelligence. My PhD (Newcastle University, 2025) focused on adversarial attack analysis and modular detection for distributed IoT systems. I am driven by one question — how do we make AI systems secure, private, and trustworthy for the humans who depend on them?

Research Focus
What I Work On

My research bridges Edge AI Security and Assistive Human-Machine Intelligence. From securing hierarchical federated learning systems against adversarial attacks, to building modular detection for IoT networks, to applying brain-computer interfaces for human communication — every thread connects to one goal: making AI systems safe for real people in the real world.

🛡️

Hierarchical Federated Learning Security

Adversarial attack analysis and discrimination scoring of client models at the edge server in distributed AI systems.

🔍

Modular Neural Networks for IoT

Lightweight, edge-deployable detection for early-stage botnet and intrusion threats in constrained IoT environments.

🧠

Brain-Computer Interface & EEG

Motor imagery signal processing and ML for sign language recognition — bridging neural signals and human communication.

👂

AI for Cochlear Implants

Applying secure, privacy-preserving edge AI to hearing assistive devices — ensuring neural data remains protected.

🌐

Edge AI Security Frameworks

End-to-end security assessment for hierarchical federated deep learning systems deployed at scale in critical infrastructure.

🏙️

Smart City & Vision 2030

Security and privacy for AI systems in large-scale IoT deployments — directly aligned with Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030.
